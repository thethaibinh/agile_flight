Parameters
depthImageThe depth image stored as a 16 bit single channel image (CV_16UC1)
depthScaleThe conversion between pixel value and the associated depth value. For example: depthInMeters = depthScale * valueStoredInPixel
focalLengthThe focal length of the camera that took the depth image.
principalPointXThe location in pixel coordinates of the center of the image horizontally (usually half the width)
principalPointYThe location in pixel coordinates of the center of the image vertically (usually half the height)
physicalVehicleRadiusThe true radius of the vehicle. Any depth values closer than this distance to the camera will be ignored. [meters]
vehicleRadiusForPlanningWe plan as if the vehicle has this radius. This value should be slightly larger than physicalVehicleRadius to account for pose estimation errors and trajectory tracking errors. [meters]
minimumCollisionDistanceWe do not perform collision checking on parts of the candidate trajectory closer than this distance to the camera. Obstacles closer than this distance can still occlude other parts of the trajectory (causing them to be labeled as in collision). This is used to enforce field of view constraints as well; we assume there is an obstacle just outside of the field of view this distance away from the camera. [meters]

◆ _pyramidSearchPixelBuffer

int RectangularPyramidPlanner::DepthImagePlanner::_pyramidSearchPixelBuffer
private

If the endpoint of a trajectory is within this many pixels of the lateral face of a pyramid, we will not use that pyramid for collision checking. This is to prevent the scenario where a trajectory intersects the lateral face of a pyramid, and then the same pyramid is repeatedly used for collision checking (entering an infinite loop).
